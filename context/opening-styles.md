# Opening Styles — Princess Bonhomme (@BehindtheSale)

The first 60 seconds of a video determine whether someone stays or bounces. These are her actual opening patterns extracted from her content.

---

## Opening style 1: The emotional anchor (her most used)

**When to use:** Any video where there's a turning-point story at the core. Especially strong for mindset/identity content and videos about a "moment that changed everything."

**Pattern:** Drop the viewer into a specific, emotionally charged moment — a line someone said, a thing that happened, a specific feeling. Let the tension sit. Then pivot to what it revealed.

**Her example:**
"You are so sweet, Princess. I'll get back to you when I'm ready."

I'm going to be honest with you. When I got off that call, I was furious.

Not at them. At myself.

Because I had done everything "right." And they ghosted me. Again.

---

## Opening style 2: Name the lie first

**When to use:** Contrarian takes, frameworks that flip conventional wisdom, any topic where the viewer has been taught something wrong. Works for tactical AND mindset content.

**Pattern:** State the wrong belief the viewer holds — flat, no hedging. Then immediately pull the rug out. "Here's what's actually true." No warm-up.

**Her example:**
Most salespeople lose deals because they think the close is the problem. It's not.

You can have the smoothest close in the world — the right words, the right reframe, the right energy — and still lose. Every time. Because the problem wasn't the close. The problem started 20 minutes before you ever pitched.

Delete the belief that you need better objection handling. That belief is keeping you broke.

---

## Opening style 3: The result-first reveal

**When to use:** Income proof videos, personal transformation content, "how I did it" style topics. Credibility-led openers that earn the right to teach.

**Pattern:** Lead with the result. Then immediately undercut the expected story. "It didn't happen the way you think."

**Her example:**
Last year, I personally collected over $3.4 million in high-ticket sales.

I was a burnt-out nursing student making $2,800 a month before I figured this out. And it did not happen the way you think it did — it didn't happen because I worked harder or got better at rebuttals. It happened because I learned to let go.

Here's what that actually looked like.

---

## Opening style 4: Direct confrontation

**When to use:** Topics where the viewer is doing something self-defeating that they may not realize. Especially strong for "why you're failing" content.

**Pattern:** Speak directly to the viewer's current behavior. No preamble. Name what they're doing, name why it's failing. Warm but zero softening.

**Her example:**
If a prospect says "let me think about it" and you say "absolutely, take all the time you need" — you just lost that deal. Not at the close. Right there. The moment you made it comfortable for them to walk away.

Being nice on sales calls is a strategy. And it's the wrong one.

---

## Opening style 5: The scenario (put them in it)

**When to use:** Educational/how-to content, tactical frameworks, any topic where the pain point is a specific, recognizable moment the viewer has lived.

**Pattern:** Build a scene that the viewer has experienced. Let it be specific. Time, place, the exact thing that happened. Then land the "that was me too" or "here's what was actually happening."

**Her example:**
Picture this.

You're on a call. You've followed the prep. You've been warm, professional, engaged. The prospect seems interested. And at the end — right when you pitch — they say: "This sounds great. I just need to think about it."

You know in that moment you've lost them. But you don't know why.

I used to live in that moment. Every single week. And I was convinced the problem was my close. It wasn't.

---

## Notes for Claude

- Her hook is almost always 2–4 short sentences before the first pause. She builds a specific moment, lets it land, then pivots.
- She never warms up the audience. No recap of who she is, no "today we're going to talk about." She opens on content.
- The strongest openers from her content are emotional anchors (Style 1) and naming the lie (Style 2). Use these first unless the topic specifically calls for Style 3 or 4.
- She uses "right?" and "my friend" as pull-through devices in the opening, not as filler.
- Conviction over cleverness. The opening doesn't need to be the most creative line — it needs to sound like she means every word.
- Never open with a question she doesn't immediately answer. She either asks and answers, or doesn't ask at all.
