# Stories Bank — Princess Bonhomme (@BehindtheSale)

Pulled from her YouTube channel content. These are real stories she has shared publicly.

---

## Story 1 — The Origin: Nursing Student to $2,800/Month
**Context:** Her before-state / origin story.
**What happened:** She was a burnt-out nursing student making $2,800/month before she got into sales. That was her starting point — not a high earner, not someone with a sales background.
**Use for:** Origin hooks, "I wasn't born into this" framing, relatability for beginners.
**Her words (paraphrase):** "From burnt-out nursing student making $2,800/month to six-figure sales rep in 3 years, all by learning to let go."

## Story 2 — Selling from Her Parents' Attic
**Context:** Her early days in sales before she had the "right setup."
**What happened:** She closed deals from her parents' attic — beige wall behind her, cheap webcam, $20 headset from Amazon. Still got the yes.
**The lesson:** Authority and conviction close deals. Production quality doesn't. "Even with my cheap webcam and my $20 headset from Amazon, I was still able to get a yes."
**Use for:** Objecting to the "I'm not ready" or "I don't have the right setup" belief. Conviction over aesthetics.

## Story 3 — "You Are So Sweet, Princess" (The Breaking Point)
**Context:** The moment that changed her approach to selling.
**What happened:** Prospects would finish calls saying "You are so sweet, Princess" — and then ghost her. The line that finally broke her: "This was a lovely conversation, Princess, but I'll get back to you when I'm ready." She was furious. She hadn't gotten into sales to be sweet. She'd gotten into it to help people.
**The lesson:** Being nice is why you're broke. Courage to say what needs to be said is what separates six-figure closers.
**Use for:** Opening hooks, the moment of change, why directness is not rudeness.
**Her words (direct quote):** "I was so mad and this was the turning point because I got this all the time and I did not get into sales to be sweet. I got into sales to help people."

## Story 4 — The Real Estate Agent Who Didn't Watch the Video
**Context:** She sold to real estate agents early in her career.
**What happened:** Prospects would show up to her calls without watching the prep video she'd emailed them. She used to be terrified to call it out — worried they'd hang up. When she finally stopped being afraid and started asking "Why didn't you watch it?", they would apologize. She'd make them watch the 7-minute video on the call, go make a coffee, come back, and by the end of the call they'd apologize again — while paying the invoice and signing the agreement.
**The lesson:** When you hold people accountable and show you have standards, they respect you more, not less.
**Use for:** Setting standards, earning respect in phase 1 of discovery, calling out the elephant.

## Story 5 — Panic Attacks Before Every Call (Before the Framework)

**Use for:** Relatability for people who feel anxious or underprepared. Before/after framework revelation.
