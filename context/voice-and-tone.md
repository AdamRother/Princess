# Voice and Tone — Princess Bonhomme (@BehindtheSale)

## On-camera energy
Direct, warm, and confident — but not performative. Mid-energy. Gets louder and more emphatic when making a point she really believes. Occasionally lets her voice crack when talking about her mission ("sometimes you hear my voice crack — this is the mission"). Spiritually grounded — references faith naturally ("praise God"). Not a hype person. Not a screamer. The energy of a mentor who is genuinely invested in the person she's talking to.

## Speaking style
Conversational and direct. Talks like she's having a real one-on-one conversation, not presenting on a stage. Addresses the viewer as "you" constantly. Uses rhetorical questions — asks them and then answers them herself, or lets them hang. Calls out the lies people tell themselves. Confronts limiting beliefs directly but warmly. Frequently ends statements with "right?" to pull the viewer along.

## Her teaching framework (use this to structure every script)

**The Pain Discovery Framework** — her named methodology. 3 phases:
- **Phase 1 (START):** Set the standard before discovery. First 90 seconds determine the call. Standard-setting = authority. Reference: real estate agent story.
- **Phase 2 (IMPROVE):** Run discovery like a detective. Built on two Truths:
  - *Truth #1: Why they're buying TODAY* — the real reason under the surface. Ask Why repeatedly until you reach the genuine motivation (not "$8k/month flexibility" but what's actually driving that). The first answer is never the whole story.
  - *Truth #2: How to deliver the difficult message* — don't tell them what to do; ask the question that helps them see it. "How long have you wanted this? What's held you back?" The skill is the mental process in YOUR mind during discovery, not just the question.
- **Phase 3 (CLOSE):** Pitch as a reflection, not a presentation. Reflect their words back as the solution. "Based on everything you shared, here's what I think changes it."

**The Motivation Matrix** — 3 types of buyers she teaches:
- *Pain Buyer:* Running away from a bad situation. Urgency is real but fragile — help them own the pain, don't amplify it.
- *Gain Buyer:* Chasing an outcome. Motivated but not urgent — help them feel the cost of NOT acting.
- *Transformation Buyer:* Buying identity, not just outcome. Help them see who they're becoming.

**The Unsell** — her specific tool when hesitation shows up at close. Instead of pushing with rebuttals, pull back: "I want to make sure this is right for you — if it's not, I'd rather you walk away today." Removes pressure, dissolves cold feet, opens the real conversation. Not a trick — it's genuine care over commission.

**Core belief about the close:** "The pitch is just a formality." When Phase 1 and 2 are done right, the prospect has already decided. The close is a confirmation, not a battle.

## Tone dos and don'ts

**She DOES sound like:**
- Direct and specific — never vague
- Someone who has lived this, not just read about it
- Warm but unafraid to challenge
- Grounded in faith without being preachy
- A peer who's a few steps ahead, not a guru on a pedestal
- Someone who calls out the truth with a smile on her face

**She does NOT sound like:**
- A motivational speaker with no specifics
- A corporate sales trainer reading from a deck
- Someone trying to be liked ("I did not get into sales to be sweet. I got into sales to help people.")
- Soft or hedging
- Using jargon for its own sake

## Pacing and rhythm
Uses short declarative sentences for impact. Then expands. Pauses before and after key points. Builds to a moment and lands it. Uses numerical specificity to build credibility ("$2.4 trillion circulated in the US alone last year, trillion with a T").
