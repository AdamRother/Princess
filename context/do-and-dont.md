# Do and Don't — Behind the Sale (@BehindtheSale)

Hard rules for every script. These override everything else.

---

## ALWAYS DO

- **Use her real stories.** Pull from stories-bank.md. Never invent experiences she hasn't had.
- **Be direct.** State the point, then explain it. Never bury the lead.
- **Name the lie first.** She structures content around exposing false beliefs before offering the truth.
- **Use "you" constantly.** She speaks directly to the viewer, not about them.
- **Include specific numbers.** Dollar amounts, percentages, timeframes. "$2,800/month," "$3.4M," "14 minutes ago," "7-minute video." Specificity = credibility.
- **Use her signature phrases naturally.** "Make it make sense," "my friend," "selling is serving," "life-changing income," "objection soup," "word tracks," "say the thing that needs to be said."
- **End with identity.** Close on who the viewer is becoming, not just what they're doing.
- **Conviction over polish.** Scripts should sound like she believes every word. Not like a deck.
- **Reference faith naturally when relevant.** "Praise God" appears in her real speech — it's authentic, not performative.

## NEVER DO

- **Never start with "Welcome back" or any greeting as the first words.** She opens on content immediately.
- **Never use the phrase "tips and tricks."** She doesn't do tips. She does frameworks and mindset shifts.
- **Never tell the viewer to "hustle harder" or "work more."** Her whole framework is about doing LESS but better (she went from $2,800 to 6-figures by "learning to let go" and "doing less").
- **Never script objection handling tactics.** Her brand is explicitly anti-objection-handling. She eliminates objections through discovery, not handles them at the end.
- **Never hedge.** No "it depends," no "some people say," no "you might find that." She states things flat.
- **Never use the word "pushy" neutrally.** To her, if you're doing it right, you're never pushy — you're direct and serving. The word "pushy" only appears to call out the lie that directness = pushy.
- **Never write word-for-word scripts for prospects.** She explicitly mocks "word tracks" — give frameworks and principles, not lines to parrot.
- **Never make the content about her competition.** She doesn't reference other coaches or methodologies as competition. She references ideas (NEPQ, Cardone, etc.) only as context, not endorsement.
- **Never make the CTA desperate.** She doesn't beg for likes or subscribes. Her CTAs are confident and service-framed.
